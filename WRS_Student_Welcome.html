Workplace Readiness Skills

The professional skills that employers say matter just as much as the technical ones — and the skills you'll be assessed on at the end of your program.

What is this course?

This course is built around Workplace Readiness Skills — also called soft skills, professional skills, people skills, or employability skills depending on who you ask. Whatever you call them, they describe the same thing: the behaviors, habits, and interpersonal abilities that determine how you show up at work.

Your CTE program is teaching you the technical skills of your chosen field — the hands-on knowledge that makes you valuable in a specific industry. This course is the complement to that. It covers the 21 skills that apply across every industry and every job, from healthcare to construction to business to technology.

Think of it this way: Technical skills get you in the door. Workplace readiness skills determine what happens after you walk through it — whether you keep the job, get promoted, or become someone your employer trusts with more responsibility.

Why do these skills matter?

This isn't just something teachers say. Employers across Nevada and across the country consistently report the same thing: they can train new hires on technical tasks, but they struggle to find candidates who already show up ready to work professionally.

The WRS assessment

📋 What you need to know

At the end of your CTE program, you will take the Workplace Readiness Skills assessment — a standardized test that measures your knowledge of all 21 standards. This course is designed specifically to prepare you for that assessment.

The assessment doesn't ask you to memorize definitions. It gives you workplace scenarios and asks you to identify the most professional response. The modules in this course — especially the Applied modules and the knowledge check quizzes — mirror that format exactly.

The more you engage with the scenarios, quizzes, and discussions in this course, the better prepared you'll be when assessment time comes.

How to navigate this course

1
Start with Foundational modules Each standard has a Short Module and an Extended Module at the Foundational level. These introduce the skill and build your baseline understanding. Start here if a standard is new to you.
2
Move to Applied modules when you're ready Applied modules push into harder scenarios and exam-style questions. These are where your preparation for the WRS assessment really happens.
3
Complete the exit ticket discussions Each module ends with a Canvas Discussion prompt. These are your chance to connect the content to your own experiences — and to hear how your classmates are thinking about the same situations.
4
Use the quizzes as practice, not just a grade The knowledge check quizzes give you instant feedback on every answer — including why wrong answers are wrong. Read that feedback. It's where a lot of the learning happens.
A note on your CTE program: The technical skills you're learning in your pathway — whether that's healthcare, construction, business, culinary arts, or something else entirely — are the foundation of your career. This course doesn't replace that. It adds to it. The most successful professionals combine strong technical ability with strong workplace readiness. That's the goal.
